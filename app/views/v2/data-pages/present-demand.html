Understanding present demand for adult social care services helps identify where needs are being met and where gaps may exist. Here you can find insights into both met and unmet demand, as well as factors that drive this.

Current demand

The number of care beds per 1,000 people allows you to compare care provision between different areas. A higher number may suggest greater demand, particularly when occupancy rates are high.

Demand can also be shaped by population need. Learn about drivers of demand.

Care homes in Suffolk have 18.4 beds per 1,000 people - 31% more than the East of England average (12.2).

Despite this higher provision, Suffolk care homes are operating at 85% occupancy, indicating that capacity may still be constrained.

In comparison, the East of England's 77% occupancy rate suggests some areas have slightly more capacity to meet demand.

Drivers of demand

Demand for adult social care is influenced by a range of factors, including the age and disability profile of your population. Areas with a higher proportion of older adults or people with disabilities typically experience greater pressure on services.

Suffolk has a larger proportion of people aged 65 and over (18%) compared to the national average (7%). This suggests demand for care services in this local authority would be higher than average.

Summary of key trends

Within the provided data, it is clear that Suffolk has a higher rate of hospital beds per 1,000 people (18.0) compared to the East of England (12.2). Also notable is the higher rate of bed occupancy in the 65 and over age group in Suffolk (75%) relative to the East of England (61%). Suffolk also shows a higher overall bed occupancy rate (82%) compared to the East of England (78%). Disability rates across both areas remain at 9%, higher than the national average of 6% for all of England.

Insight 1: High Bed Occupancy in Older Adults in Suffolk

Suffolk shows an elevated bed occupancy rate of 75% for individuals aged 65 and over, which is higher compared to the East of Englandâ€™s 61%. This suggests that the demand for care services for older adults in Suffolk is relatively high.

How you might use this insight

  • Planning for increased capacity in adult social care services specifically targeting the elderly could be vital in Suffolk. This could involve investing in more residential care homes or additional supportive housing aimed at seniors.
  • Developing specialised health and wellness programmes aimed at maintaining the health and independence of older adults might help in reducing the high bed occupancy rates.

Insight 2: Disability Rates Consistent with Region but Higher than National Average

Disability rates in Suffolk and the East of England are both at 9%, which is consistent within the region but higher than the national average of 6% for all of England. This could indicate a need for targeted support for disabled adults within these regions.

How you might use this insight

  • A potential strategy might include implementing more comprehensive community-based services and support systems that address the specific needs of individuals with disabilities, ensuring they have access to day-to-day activities and health services.
  • Investing in training for adult social care professionals on best practices in disability management might improve the quality of care and outcomes for people with disabilities in these regions.

Beds per 1,000 people
    Details for Beds per 1,000 people
    What this measures The total number of beds recorded by care providers across health and adult social care, adjusted to a rate of 1,000 people in the population.
    Data source Capacity Tracker (opens in new tab)
    Data last updated 5 February 2025
    Update frequency Daily
    Date range From 30 September 2017 to 5 February 2025.
    Methodology The total number of beds is the number of beds reported by care providers, including those in use and those available for new admissions.

    Total beds includes mental health, learning disabilities, and end-of-life care beds in residential and nursing care homes.The data covers both self-funded and local authority-funded beds.

    Care providers update this information daily or whenever changes occur using the Capacity Tracker tool. They report how many beds are available and occupied in care settings such as nursing homes, residential care, and community care.

    The data is grouped by local areas, regions, and nationally so it is easier to compare. The information is checked and updated regularly to make sure it is as accurate as possible.
    Limitations Data is self-reported.
