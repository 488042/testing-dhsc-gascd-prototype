Drivers of population needs

Population needs for adult social care are influenced by a range of factors, including the population's age structure, disability profile and rate of dementia diagnosis.

Areas with a higher proportion of older adults, people with disabilities or rates of dementia diagnosis typically experience greater pressure on services.

Suffolk has a larger proportion of people aged 65 and over (18%) compared to the national average (7%). This suggests the need for care services adjusted by population size in this local authority may be higher than average.

Current capacity - care homes: local authority-level insights

The number of adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population provides an indicator of current care capacity. A higher number suggests more sufficient capacity.

Care homes in Suffolk have 18.4 beds per 100,000 adult population, compared to the East of England regional average of 12.2 per 100,000.

Suffolk care homes are operating at 85% occupancy, which is higher than the regional average (77%). This suggests a more limited capacity to meet population needs than the regional average.

Current capacity – care homes: care provider-level insights

Examining individual care providers offers insight into how their capacity compares with other care providers at local authority, regional and national level.

Care Provider A is a small provider with 20 total beds, compared to the median (53 beds) in Suffolk.

Care Provider A has 90% occupancy for all adult social care beds compared to the median occupancy of all care homes in Suffolk (85%), suggesting limited availability for new admissions.
