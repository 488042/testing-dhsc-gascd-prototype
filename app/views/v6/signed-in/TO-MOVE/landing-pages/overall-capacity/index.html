2. Unpaid care

Unpaid care is provided for many reasons, including when formal adult social care services are unavailable or do not meet individual needs.

This table shows the percentage of people providing unpaid care at different levels each week.

3. Capacity in care home sector

Adult social care beds and percentage of occupancy are used as measures of capacity in the care home sector.

3.1 Adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population

The number of adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population provides an indicator of current care capacity. A higher number usually suggests more sufficient capacity for locations with similar needs.

Indicator Suffolk East of England All England

General nursing beds per 100,000 adult population 412 335 299

3.2 Percentage of occupied adult social care beds

Bed occupancy levels can show how much current care capacity is being used. High occupancy may mean limited availability to meet needs.

4. Capacity in domiciliary care sector

Domiciliary care capacity can be measured by the number of care provider locations providing domiciliary care, service users, paid workforce hours, and workers employed in the sector.

This is adjusted by the size of adult population for comparing between different geographical areas.

Indicator Suffolk East of England All England
Domiciliary care providers per 100,000 adult population 24.1 21 19.5
People receiving domiciliary care per 100,000 adult population 640 691 880
Workforce hours paid per week (agency workers included) per person receiving domiciliary care 62.3 59.2 55.3
Domiciliary care workers (agency workers included) per 100,000 adult population 1,293 1,220 1,443

Source: Capacity Tracker
Data correct as of 5 February 2025
