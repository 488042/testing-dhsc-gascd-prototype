Summary of key trends

The number of people aged 85 and over is increasing across England, with some areas seeing a much faster rise. Between 2015 and 2023, this age group grew by over 60% in parts of Suffolk, East Suffolk, and Cambridgeshire, compared to a national increase of around 30%. This trend suggests increasing need for adult social care services, particularly in regions with rapid population ageing.

Insight 1: Suffolk and Cambridgeshire have the highest proportions of people aged 85 and over

Suffolk and Cambridgeshire stand out as having a greater proportion of older residents compared to the England average (3.1%) and the East of England average (2.9%). In Suffolk, 3.3% of the population is aged 85 and over, and in Cambridgeshire, it is slightly higher at 3.5%. This suggests that local social care services may need to adapt to meet the needs of an ageing population.

How you might use this insight

  • Assess whether social care funding in Suffolk and Cambridgeshire reflects the higher proportion of older people.
  • Consider workforce planning to ensure there are enough care staff to support the increasing number of older residents.
