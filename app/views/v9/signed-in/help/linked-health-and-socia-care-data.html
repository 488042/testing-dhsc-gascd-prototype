What this measures
The linked data insights combine health and social care data across seven identified acute healthcare cohorts to show:

  • the size of the cohort at risk of needing long-term adult social care arranged for by the local authority
  • the percentage of people within each cohort that went on to receive long-term adult social care arranged for by the local authority within 12 months of an acute hospital event
Source
Hospital Episode Statistics (HES), Emergency Care Dataset (ECDS) and Client Level Data (CLD)
Data correct as of
31 March 2025
Update frequency
Yearly (Financial Year)
Methodology

For each of the seven cohorts:

Cohort 1: Adults with severe mental health conditions and frailty

Adults 18+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2023/24 financial year as an emergency with a recorded diagnosis of a severe mental health condition and severe frailty.

  • Severe mental health condition defined by having one of the following diagnosis codes in any position: F20, F21, F22, F25, F28, F29, F30, F31
  • Severe frailty defined by having a Hospital Frailty Risk Score (HFRS) greater than 15

Cohort 2: Adults with dementia admitted in an emergency

Adults 18+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2023/24 financial year as an emergency with a recorded diagnosis of dementia.

  • Dementia defined by having one of the following diagnosis codes in any position: F00, F01, F02, F03, F05, G30, R41

Cohort 3: Adults with learning disability admitted in an emergency

Adults 18+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2023/24 financial year as an emergency with a recorded diagnosis of a learning disability.

  • Learning disability defined by having one of the following diagnosis codes in any position: F067, F70, F71, F72, F73, F74, F75, F76, F77, F78, F79, F803, F813, F818, F819, F83, F84, F88, F89

Cohort 4: Adults with severe mental health conditions and frequent emergency admissions

Adults 18+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2022/23 financial year as an emergency with a recorded diagnosis of a severe mental health condition and a history of multiple emergency admissions in the same year.

  • Severe mental health condition defined by having one of the following diagnosis codes in any position: F20, F21, F22, F25, F28, F29, F30, F31

Cohort 5: Adults with complex physical disabilities admitted in an emergency

Adults 18+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2022/23 financial year as an emergency with a recorded diagnosis of complex physical disabilities and a history of multiple emergency admissions in the same year.

  • Complex physical disability defined by having one of the following diagnosis codes in any position: G80, G81, G82, G83, G35, G60, G61, G62, G63, G64, G70, G71, G72, G73, S06, T90, I60, I61, I62, I63, I64, G20, H54, H91

Cohort 6: Adults 65+ with severe frailty admitted in an emergency

Adults 65+ admitted into an acute general hospital in the 2023/24 financial year as an emergency with severe frailty.

  • Severe frailty defined by having a Hospital Frailty Risk Score (HFRS) greater than 15

Cohort 7: Adults 85+ with multiple A&E attendances

Adults 85+ who have multiple A&E attendances in the 2023/24 financial year.

Two metrics were calculated for each cohort as follows:

The size of the cohort at risk of needing long-term adult social care arranged for by the local authority

Calculated as the number of people within each cohort that met the criteria for the cohort. Note a hierarchy is applied to the cohorts and so if an individual meets the criteria for more than one cohort they will fall into the highest ranked cohort only. The hierarchy was based on the client level data extracts and decided with an expert group of local authority commissioners, analysts, clinicians and social care practitioners.

The percentage of people within each cohort that went on to receive long-term adult social care arranged for by the local authority within 12 months of an acute hospital event

Calculated as the number of people within each cohort who go on to start a new long-term care package arranged for by the local authority within 12 months of an acute hospital event divided by the total number of people within each cohort.
