1. Introduction

Adult social care provision includes different types of care, such as unpaid care, care homes and domiciliary care.

Each type of care plays a role in meeting local care needs, and the level of pressure and availability may vary across different types of care.

For more information on capacity, check the latest Market Position Statement and Joint Strategic Needs Assessment documents. You will usually find these on the local authority's website.

6. Capacity in care home sector

Adult social care beds and percentage of occupancy are used as measures of capacity in the care home sector.

  • adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population

Adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population

The number of adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population provides an indicator of current care capacity. A higher number usually suggests more sufficient capacity for locations with similar needs.

7. Capacity in domiciliary care sector

Domiciliary care capacity can be measured by the number of care provider locations providing domiciliary care, service users, paid workforce hours, and workers employed in the sector.
