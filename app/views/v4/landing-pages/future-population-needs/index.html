4. Demographic factors driving population needs

Population needs for adult social care are shaped by a range of factors, including age, income levels, employment status, disability, long-term health conditions, and household composition.

Areas with higher proportions of older adults, people with disabilities, or long-term health conditions typically experience greater pressure on services.

4.4 Future population aged 65 and over needing domestic help

Domestic tasks are important for independent living but not essential for basic functioning. These include housework, shopping for food, managing paperwork or bills, and getting out of the house. These tasks are known as Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADLs).

4.5 Future population aged 65 and over needing self-care help

Self-care tasks are essential daily activities related to personal care and mobility. These include bathing, dressing, using the toilet, eating, taking medication, and moving around indoors. These tasks are known as Activities of Daily Living (ADLs).
