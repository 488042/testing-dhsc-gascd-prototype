4. Demographic factors driving population needs

Population needs for adult social care are shaped by a range of factors, including age, income levels, employment status, disability, long-term health conditions, and household composition.

Areas with higher proportions of older adults, people with disabilities, or long-term health conditions typically experience greater pressure on services.

Economic factors also play a significant role in determining the level and type of care individuals need.

5. Overall capacity at local authority level

Adult social care provision includes different types of care, such as unpaid care, care homes and domiciliary care.

Each type of care plays a role in meeting local care needs, and the level of pressure and availability may vary across different types of care.

  • unpaid care
  • capacity in care home sector
  • capacity in domiciliary care sector

5.2 Capacity in care home sector

Adult social care beds and percentage of occupancy are used as measures of capacity in the care home sector.

Adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population

The number of adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population provides an indicator of current care capacity. A higher number usually suggests more sufficient capacity for locations with similar needs.
