Smart insights (experimental)

These insights are powered by artificial intelligence (AI), which can sometimes be inaccurate. Verify the information with primary sources before sharing or using it. Learn more about smart insights

Insight 1

The proportion of people in Suffolk who feel safe and secure has risen steadily, reaching 89.4% in 2023 compared to 82.5% in 2017. This trend highlights improvements in creating a sense of safety and trust for clients receiving care.

How you might use this insight:

  • Build on what works: Identify and expand practices or services that have contributed to this improvement, such as enhanced safeguarding protocols or better communication with clients.
  • Maintain momentum: Ensure resources and training remain focused on safety and security, particularly for vulnerable groups such as older adults and females.

Insight 2

Clients aged 65 and over report improving safety levels, rising from 82% in 2022 to 88.8% in 2023. However, this group continues to report slightly lower safety levels compared to clients aged 18 to 64, whose safety perception reached 90.5% in 2023.

How you might use this insight:

  • Tailor safety initiatives for older clients: Enhance safety measures and support for clients aged 65 and over, addressing their unique concerns, which may include physical safety, accessiblity or in-home care.
  • Close the safety perception gap: Apply strategies that have driven higher safety perceptions in younger clients to improve outcomes for older adults.
