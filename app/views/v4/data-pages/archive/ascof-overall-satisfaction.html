Smart insights (experimental)

These insights are powered by artificial intelligence (AI), which can sometimes be inaccurate. Verify the information with primary sources before sharing or using it. Learn more about smart insights

Insight 1

Overall satisfaction with care and support in Suffolk has fluctuated, with a significant drop in 2020 (62.8%) and partial recovery to 67.4% in 2023. This suggests satisfaction levels are still below pre-pandemic figures, which peaked at 69.1% in 2019.

How you might use this insight:

  • Investigate satisfaction trends: Conduct client surveys to explore factors contributing to the drop in satisfaction and the slow recovery.
  • Target key improvements: Focus on areas such as communication, care accessibility, and service delivery to bring satisfaction levels closer to pre-pandemic highs.

Insight 2

Satisfaction levels for clients aged 65 and over remain lower (64.9% in 2023) compared to the 18 to 64 age group, which saw a significant improvement to 71.4% in 2023. This indicates older clients may face unique challenges affecting their overall satisfaction.

How you might use this insight:

  • Enhance services for older clients: Tailor support to address specific needs of clients aged 65 and over, such as improving accessibility and fostering greater engagement.
  • Analyse age-specific feedback: Collect and compare feedback from different age groups to identify targeted strategies for increasing satisfaction among older adults.
