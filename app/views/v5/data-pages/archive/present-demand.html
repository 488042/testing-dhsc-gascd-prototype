Drivers of demand

Demand for adult social care is influenced by a range of factors, including the age structure, disability profile and rate of dementia diagnosis in your population.

Areas with a higher proportion of older adults, people with disabilities or rates of dementia diagnosis typically experience greater pressure on services.

Suffolk has a larger proportion of people aged 65 and over (18%) compared to the national average (7%). This suggests demand for care services adjusted by population size in this local authority may be higher than average.

Current provision - care homes: local authority-level insights

The number of adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population provides an indicator of current care provision. A higher number suggests more sufficient provision.

Care homes in Suffolk have 18.4 beds per 100,000 people, compared to the East of England regional average of 12.2 per 100,000.

Despite a higher provision, Suffolk care homes are operating at 85% occupancy, exceeding the regional average (77%) and national average (75%). This suggests a more limited capacity to meet demand than the regional and national average.
