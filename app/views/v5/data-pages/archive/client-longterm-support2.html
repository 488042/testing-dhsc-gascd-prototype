Insight 1

Total quality of life scores in Suffolk have gradually declined, dropping from 19.7 in 2018 to 18.9 in 2023. The 2022 score of 18.8 was the lowest recorded, highlighting a continued downward trend in recent years.

How you might use this insight:

  • Investigate contributing factors: Conduct in-depth analysis to identify specific issues impacting overall quality of life, such as access to services, social connections, or health support.
  • Focus on improvement initiatives: Develop targeted interventions to improve areas like social engagement, mental health support, and access to tailored care services.

Insight 2

The quality of life for individuals aged 65 and over has consistently been lower than for the 18 to 64 age group, with no improvement from 18.7 in both 2022 and 2023. This suggests older adults face persistent challenges in maintaining their quality of life compared to younger populations.

How you might use this insight:

  • Tailor services for older adults: Prioritise resources and programmes that address the specific needs of clients aged 65 and over, such as mobility assistance, health management, and social inclusion initiatives.
  • Gather targeted feedback: Engage older clients to understand their challenges and use this feedback to inform service improvements and policy adjustments.
