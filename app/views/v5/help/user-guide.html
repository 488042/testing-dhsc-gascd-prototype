About this service

Get adult social care data is a service providing access to a range of datasets relating to adult social care in England.

How you can use this service

You can use this service to:

  • access local, regional and national data on adult social care capacity and population needs
  • filter and explore data - for example, by care type or local authority area

Data sources and accuracy

Public data sources include:

  • Adult Social Care Statistics (Department for Health and Social Care)
  • Adult Social Care Outcomes Framework (ASCOF) (NHS England)
  • Adult Social Care Report (Department for Health and Social Care)
  • Care Quality Commission (CQC) Care Directory
  • Office for National Statistics collection
  • Short and Long Term Return (SALT) (NHS England)

Private data sources include:

  • Capacity Tracker
  • Skills for Care
  • Client Level Data (CLD)

For further information on the datasets, check the official documentation from the data provider. You can find links to data providers in the sources listed with each table, chart and map visualisation in the service.

Accuracy of the data in the service

The service will be updated whenever new datasets are published.

You might find gaps or variations in the data. This is due to differences in:

  • coverage - some datasets may not include all local authorities or demographic groups
  • methodology - the way data is collected varies between datasets and this can make it difficult to make direct comparisons
  • data currency - there can be a delay between data collection and publication, also some datasets are updated less frequently
  • confidence levels - some indicators are based on sample data or estimates (these have been marked as estimates in the service)

Data indicators used in the service

These pages contain detailed information about the data indicators used in the service:

  • Adult social care beds per 100,000 adult population
  • Percentage of adult social care beds occupied
  • Number of adult social care beds in care provider location
  • People providing unpaid care, by hours per week
  • Domiciliary care providers per 100,000 adult population
  • People receiving domiciliary care per 100,000 adult population
  • Workforce hours paid per person receiving domiciliary care
  • Domiciliary care workers per 100,000 adult population

You can also find links to the relevant indicator information with the data tables, charts and map visualisations in the service.
